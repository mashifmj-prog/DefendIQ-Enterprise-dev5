DEFENDIQ ENTERPRISE ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

Effective Date: November 1, 2024

1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to establish guidelines for the appropriate use of DefendIQ Enterprise information assets including computer systems, email, internet access, and data resources.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all contractors, employees, temporary staff, and other workers at DefendIQ Enterprise, including all personnel affiliated with third parties that utilize DefendIQ Enterprise information assets.

3. POLICY STATEMENTS

3.1 General Use and Ownership

  • Users should have no expectation of privacy in anything they create, store, send or receive on the company's computer systems.
  • The company reserves the right to monitor, inspect, copy, review and store at any time and without notice any and all usage of the computer systems and any and all information.
  • Users are responsible for exercising good judgment regarding the reasonableness of personal use.

3.2 Security and Proprietary Information

  • Keep passwords secure and do not share accounts.
  • Automated external client backups of critical data must be approved by the IT Department.
  • All computers used by the contractor that connect to the DefendIQ Enterprise network must comply with the Minimum Anti-Virus Standards.

3.3 Unacceptable Use

The following activities are prohibited:

  • Violating any person's privacy rights
  • Infringing on any intellectual property rights
  • Accessing, downloading, or distributing offensive, obscene, or discriminatory material
  • Introducing malicious software into the network
  • Circumventing user authentication or security of any host, network or account

4. POLICY COMPLIANCE

Compliance with this policy will be verified through various methods, including internal and external audits. Any contractor found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of contract.

5. RELATED STANDARDS, POLICIES AND PROCESSES

  • Information Security Policy
  • Data Classification Policy
  • Remote Access Policy

6. DEFINITIONS AND TERMS

The following definition and terms can be found in the SANS Security Policy Glossary:

  • Information Assets
  • Proprietary Information
  • Security Incident
